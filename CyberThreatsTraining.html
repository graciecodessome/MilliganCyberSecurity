How Could A Cyber Attack Happen to You?

  • Leaving unintended devices in public areas. People can access your files and information
  • Shoulder Surfing. Onlooking attackers can look over your shoulder to see your login information. This threat is why it is important that passwords typed are shown as dots
  • Downloading Malwalre disguised as valid content. Be careful what you download and that you know the source. Use document checkers whenever neccessary to verify downloaded material is what the site claims them to be.
  • Phishing Scams (Fake Emails) Phishing scams are when someone sends you a fake email. The email could ask you for personal information or tempt you to download malware.
    Be careful of emails that contain
  • misspelled words
  • asking for urgent response
  • News that seems to good to be true
  • suspicious hyperlinks. You can hover over a hyperlink to see where the link takes you
  • Weak Passwords.
    A twelve character password containing only numbers can take seconds to crack, but a 14 character password with a combination of letter, numbers, and special characters can take millions of years.
