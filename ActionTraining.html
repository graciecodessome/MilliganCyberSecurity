What Can You Do to Prevent A Cyber Attack

HTTPS vs HTTP sites

If you notice in your url on websites that you are on, they typically start with either an "http" or an "https" Websites that start with https are "secure" because they use a "secure socket layer certificate" to protect your information. Your information is encrypted as it passes from your browser to the websites server. This means that hackers cannot see your data as it is on route to the webiste's server.
Try to use as many https sites as you can. If you see one of your sites is only http, do not put personal information into that site.

Scan Your Devices

There are many antivirus softwares out there that you can use to scan your computer for any viruses, malware, or potential threats. A good one to use is "Malwarebytes." These softwares can show you if your computer has any threats or has been impacted by cyber attacks, allowing you to take action and lessen the effect. MORE ON THIS?

Good Passwords

Having strong passwords can help prevent hackers from accessing your personal information.
When creating passwords, you should
  • Make unique passwords for different websites
  • use a mixture of capital and lowercase letters
  • Incorporate numbers and special characters
  • Do not use personal information in passwords
  • The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency recommends that all passwords should be atleast 16 characters in length
  • Do not share passwords
